What we do in Bonsall

Bonsall jobs skew rural. Big slab footprints, attached casitas, horse property garages that the owner wants converted to a clean polished concrete shop floor. We see a lot of solid hardwood requests in the older homes near San Luis Rey River and engineered plank in the newer Bonsall Hilltop and Saddle Creek builds.

Last fall we put down 1,800 square feet of 7 inch white oak in a hilltop home off Camino del Rey. The slab tested at 4.2 pounds of moisture vapor on a Calcium Chloride test, which is over the 3 pound threshold for most engineered manufacturers. We rolled an epoxy moisture barrier before the underlayment.

Why slab moisture matters in Bonsall

Most Bonsall homes built after 1995 sit on post-tension slabs that were poured fast during the building boom. They cure for years. We have pulled relative humidity probes out of 12-year-old slabs reading 78 percent.

And every solid hardwood failure we have ever fixed in this zip code traces back to skipped moisture testing. We do a 72-hour in-situ probe on every job, no exceptions.

  • Polished concrete in horse property garages and detached workshops
  • Metallic epoxy in pool houses and outdoor kitchen patios
  • Solid hardwood refinish in 1970s and 1980s homes near Bonsall Bridge

Local conditions that change how we install

Bonsall hits over 100 degrees in August and drops into the 30s on winter nights. Inside the house the swing is smaller, but it still moves wood. We acclimate every plank for a minimum of 5 days inside the home before we lay it.

The other Bonsall thing: avocado and citrus groves mean a lot of dust during harvest and pruning. We tape off the work zone and run negative-pressure air scrubbers on every dust-producing day so the rest of the house stays clean.

What it costs in Bonsall
