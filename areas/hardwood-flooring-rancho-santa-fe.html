Hardwood Flooring in Rancho Santa Fe

We install solid and engineered hardwood across Rancho Santa Fe, from the Covenant down through Fairbanks Ranch and out toward Cielo. Most homes we touch here sit on slab or on raised foundations with crawl spaces that swing 10 to 15% in relative humidity between June gloom and October Santa Ana season.

What We See in Rancho Santa Fe Homes

Engineered oak is the right call for nearly every Covenant property we estimate. Solid plank can work, but only with a vapor barrier and a slab moisture reading under 3 lbs per 1,000 sq ft per 24 hours. Most slabs we test in 92067 come back at 4 to 6 lbs without remediation. That is not a hardwood-ready number.

Wide plank dominates the market here. 7-inch and 9-inch engineered planks make up most of our recent installs in this zip. Anything wider than 10 inches needs a glue-and-nail assist on raised floors and full glue down on slab.

Pricing in This Zip

For 1,000 sq ft of engineered European white oak, install and material together, we typically land between $14,500 and $22,000 depending on plank width, finish grade, and prep work. Old tile demo runs another $2 to $4 per square foot. Subfloor leveling, when needed, adds $1.50 to $3 per foot.

The Covenant has material approval requirements through the Association. We have worked with them twice to push wide-plank submissions through. The process adds about 3 weeks to the project timeline.

Local Conditions That Affect Hardwood Here

Coastal influence reaches this far inland. Homes near Lago Lindo and the western half of the Covenant see overnight humidity spikes that wood acclimates to. We rest material on site for 14 days minimum here, sometimes 21 days for raised-floor homes.

Anything off El Camino del Norte or up toward Del Dios Highway sits dryer. Different acclimation curve. We track every install with a Wagner pin moisture meter and log readings on day 1, day 7, and install day. Those logs go to the homeowner with the invoice.

What We Will Not Install

Solid hardwood over a slab without a moisture mitigation barrier. We have seen too many cupped floors from contractors who skipped that step. The repair cost is usually 2x the original install. Not worth the savings.

Bamboo. We stopped quoting bamboo flooring in 2022 after three customer complaints about hardness inconsistency between batches from the same supplier.
